Introduction

The Vijayanagara empire, also known as the Karnata Kingdom, was founded in 1336. In the wake if the rebellions against Tughluq in the Deccan, The Hindu empire lasted for more than two centuries. Its history and fortunes were shaped by the increasing militarization of Peninsular politics after the Muslim invasions and the propitiation that made southern India a major participant in the trade network linking Europe and East Asia. Urbanization and monetization of the economy were the two other significant developments of the period that brought all the peninsular kingdoms into highly competitive political and military activities in the race for supremacy.
